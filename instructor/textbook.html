Chapter 2

Japan's Drone Rules

2.1 Registration and Remote ID

In Japan, every drone of 100 grams or more must be registered (登録 touroku). The registration number must be displayed on the aircraft, and the drone must broadcast Remote ID. Registration is done online through the government system DIPS 2.0.

2.2 Specified flights (特定飛行 tokutei hikou)

Some flights need government permission or approval. They are called specified flights. There are two groups:

Airspace (where)Method (how)
• Near airports
• Above 150 m
• Over DID — Densely Inhabited Districts (most city areas)
• Emergency operation airspace		 • Night flight
• BVLOS — beyond visual line of sight
• Within 30 m of people/objects
• Over events, dangerous goods, dropping objects

2.3 The three risk categories

CategoryMeaningWhat you need
INot a specified flightNo procedure (follow basic rules)
IISpecified flight with a safety zone that keeps other people out (立入管理措置 tachiiri kanri sochi)Permission/approval. A Second-Class license + certified aircraft can simplify or remove some procedures
IIISpecified flight over third parties, without a safety zone — highest riskFirst-Class license + first-class certified aircraft + approval
Remember: "Third parties" (第三者 daisansha) means people who are not involved in your flight operation. Category III means flying over them.

2.4 Basic rules for every flight

No alcohol or drugs. Always do a pre-flight check. Do not fly in a dangerous way. Give way to manned aircraft. Maximum altitude 150 m. For specified flights: make a flight plan notification, keep a flight logbook (飛行日誌 hikou nisshi), and report accidents to the government.

⚠ Penalties are real. Flying drunk, or flying over a DID without permission, can bring criminal penalties and license suspension. Rules protect you and others.

Chapter 4

Know Your Aircraft

4.1 Multirotor basics

A multirotor flies by changing the speed of each motor. Four controls:

Stick inputMovementWord you will hear
ThrottleUp / down"Climb" / "Descend"
ElevatorForward / backward"Forward" / "Backward"
AileronLeft / right"Left" / "Right"
RudderRotate (yaw)"Turn the nose"

4.2 GPS mode vs. ATTI mode

With position stabilization ON (GPS + vision sensors), the drone holds its position. With stabilization OFF ("ATTI mode"), the drone drifts with the wind — you must correct it constantly. The exam tests ATTI flying because real emergencies can disable GPS.

First-class students: ALL basic subjects are flown with stabilization OFF. Most of your training time will be ATTI practice. This is normal — everyone finds it hard at first.

4.3 Battery safety (LiPo)

Do not use a swollen or damaged battery. Store at 50–60% charge, not full. Cold weather reduces power. Carry batteries in a fireproof bag. Never throw them in normal trash.

4.4 Fail-safe / Return-to-Home (RTH)

Before every flight, check: RTH altitude (higher than obstacles!), home point updated, and the signal-lost behavior (hover / land / RTH). Wrong RTH settings cause many accidents.

Chapter 5

Inspections and Records

5.1 Pre-flight inspection — say it out loud!

In the exam, you perform the inspection and record the results on the daily inspection sheet (日常点検記録 nichijou tenken kiroku). Time limit: about 12 minutes for the two inspection subjects. Practice this order:

#CheckCall-out
1Airspace & surroundings"Airspace and surroundings — clear."
2Weather / wind"Wind is under five meters per second."
3Propellers, frame, ID plate"No damage."
4Screws & connectors"Tight."
5Power system"Normal."
6Signal & GPS"Normal."
7Battery level"Enough."
8Remote ID"Working."
9Motors"Normal."
10Control check in hover"Normal."
⚠ Exam rule: If you check safety but do not say it to the examiner, it counts as NOT checked — minus 5 points. Always call out.

5.2 After the flight

Post-flight inspection (heat, damage, dirt) + fill in the flight log: date, place, times, and total flight time. Time limit: 5 minutes. A mistake in the flight-time calculation costs 10 points — check your math!

5.3 Accidents and serious incidents — memorize the three

Accident (事故 jiko): ① injury or death of a person ② damage to property ③ collision or contact with a manned aircraft.
Serious incident (重大インシデント): ① near-collision with a manned aircraft ② injury (minor) ③ loss of control ④ fire/smoke in flight.
If an accident happens: stop the flight → help injured people → report to MLIT.
Chapter 6

The Practical Exam

6.1 How scoring works

You start each subject with 100 points. Mistakes remove points. You pass with 70+ (Second-Class) or 80+ (First-Class).

ResultExamples
INSTANT FAILCrash・hit an object (pylon, flag, net)・lose control・ dangerous speed (about 5 m/s)・one hand on the controller・enter the red zone (disqualification zone, half of the aircraft)・over the time limit・examiner takes control・ outside help・(BVLOS test) looking at the drone without instruction
−5 pointsEnter the yellow zone (deduction zone)・wrong procedure or direction・hard landing・not watching the drone・no safety call-out・moving before instruction・ clearly wrong altitude・(1st class) pirouette faster than 16 s or slower than 26 s
−1 pointWobble・rough control (sudden speed/stop)・wrong nose direction (brief)
💡 The 2-second rule: the first time you drift into the yellow zone on a route segment, you lose no points if you return to the route within about 2 seconds after the examiner tells you. Recover fast, stay calm.
💡 Wind gusts: if the wind pushes your drone and you correct it quickly and properly, there is no deduction. Do not panic.

6.2 The maneuvers

Subject2nd Class (basic)1st Class (basic)
Square flightGPS ON, 3.5 m, 8 minGPS OFF, climb 1.5→3.5 m, 6 min
Figure-eightGPS ON, 1.5 m, 2 laps, 8 minGPS OFF, 1+ lap, then emergency landing, 5 min
Pirouette (360° hover turn)—GPS OFF, 3.5 m, one turn in ~20 s (16–26 s), 3 min

6.3 Emergency landing procedure

When the examiner says "Emergency!": ① stop and hover immediately → ② wait for the landing instruction → ③ keep your altitude and fly the shortest route to the landing point → ④ land softly inside the 2 m circle.

Chapter 8

Check Yourself — 10 Questions

  1. From what weight must a drone be registered in Japan?
    → 100 grams.
  2. Name the three airspace types that make a flight "specified".
    → Near airports / above 150 m / over DID (+ emergency operation airspace).
  3. What is Category III?
    → A specified flight over third parties without a safety zone. Needs a First-Class license and a first-class certified aircraft.
  4. What is the pass line of the Second-Class practical exam?
    → 70 points out of 100 (First-Class: 80).
  5. You enter the yellow zone for the first time on a segment. What must you do to avoid losing points?
    → Return to the route within about 2 seconds after the examiner tells you.
  6. Name three instant-fail actions.
    → e.g. crash, hitting an object, losing control, dangerous speed (~5 m/s), one-hand control, entering the red zone, over the time limit.
  7. What are the three "accidents" you must report?
    → Injury/death of a person, damage to property, collision or contact with a manned aircraft.
  8. Why does the exam use ATTI mode (stabilization OFF)?
    → To prove you can control the drone safely when GPS fails in a real emergency.
  9. What must you do when you finish a safety check?
    → Say it out loud to the examiner. Silent checks count as not done (−5).
  10. How long is the license valid, and what language is the national knowledge test?
    → 3 years; Japanese only.
